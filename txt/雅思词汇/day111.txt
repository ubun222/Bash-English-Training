You say by contrast or in contrast, or in contrast to something, to show that you are mentioning a very different situation from the one you have just mentioned. 相比之下
A contrast is a great difference between two or more things which is clear when you compare them. (巨大的) 差别
contrast |英 ['kɒntrɑːst]  美 ['kɑntræst]| vi. 对比；形成对照/vt. 使对比；使与…对照/n. 对比；差别；对照物/
...a television with brighter colours, better contrast, and digital sound.…一台色彩更亮丽、颜色对比度更好、配有数字音响的电视。
Johnson's easy charm contrasted sharply with the prickliness of his boss.约翰的随和与他老板的挑剔形成了鲜明的对比。
Contrast that approach with what goes on in most organizations.对比这个方法与大多数组织运用的方法的不同之处。
She contrasted the situation then with the present crisis.她把过去的情况与现在的危机进行了对比。
His public statements have always been in marked contrast to those of his son.他在公共场合的发言总是和他儿子的话截然不同。
In contrast, the lives of girls in well-to-do families were often very sheltered.相比之下，富裕家庭的女孩子们通常都过着养尊处优的生活。
The private sector, by contrast, has plenty of money to spend.私营部门相比之下有很多钱可花。
The two visitors provided a startling contrast in appearance.两位来访者的外貌有惊人的不同。
...the contrast between town and country.…城乡差别。


If you say that something is presumably the case, you mean that you think it is very likely to be the case, although you are not certain. 很可能
presumably |英 [prɪ'zjuːməblɪ]  美 [prɪ'zuməbli]| adv. 大概；推测起来；可假定/
The spear is presumably the murder weapon.这矛很可能就是凶器。


A conservatory is an institution where musicians are trained. 音乐学院
conservatory |英 [kən'sɜːvət(ə)rɪ]  美 [kən'sɝvətɔri]| n. 温室；音乐学校/adj. 有保存力的；保存性的/
...the New England Conservatory of Music.…新英格兰音乐学院。


embedded 根深蒂固的
If something such as an attitude or feeling is embedded in a society or system, or in someone's personality, it becomes a permanent and noticeable feature of it. 使根深蒂固
embedded 嵌入的
If an object embeds itself in a substance or thing, it becomes fixed there firmly and deeply. 嵌入
embed |英 [ɪm'bed; em-]  美 [ɪm'bɛd]| vt. 栽种；使嵌入，使插入；使深留脑中/
I think that hatred of the other is deeply embedded in our society.我觉得对异己的憎恨在我们的社会中根深蒂固。
This agreement will be embedded in a state treaty to be signed soon.这项协议将被纳入到一份即将被签署的国家条约中。
The fossils at Dinosaur Cove are embedded in hard sandstone.恐龙湾中的化石嵌在坚硬的砂岩中。
One of the bullets passed through Andrea's chest before embedding itself in a wall.其中一颗子弹穿过安德烈亚的胸膛然后嵌入一面墙中。


Someone who is available is not busy and is therefore free to talk to you or to do a particular task. 有空的
availability 可获得性
If something you want or need is available, you can find it or obtain it. 可获得的
available |英 [ə'veɪləb(ə)l]  美 [ə'veləbl]| adj. 可获得的；可购得的；可找到的；有空的/
Mr. Leach is on holiday and was not available for comment.利奇先生在休假，没空作评论。
...the easy availability of guns.…获取枪支的容易性。
The shop has about 500 autographed copies of the book available for purchase.这家书店有大约500本该书的签名版本可供购买。
Since 1978, the amount of money available to buy books has fallen by 17%.自1978年以来，可供买书的钱已减少了17%。


If you describe a place or the weather as arctic, you are emphasizing that it is extremely cold. 极冷的
The Arctic is the area of the world around the North Pole. It is extremely cold and there is very little light in winter and very little darkness in summer. 北极
Arctic |英 ['ɑːktɪk]  美 ['ɑrktɪk]| adj. 北极的；极寒的/n. 北极圈；御寒防水套鞋/n. (Arctic)人名；(英)阿克蒂克/
The bathroom, with its spartan pre-war facilities, is positively arctic.这个有简陋战前设施的浴室实在冷极了。
...winter in the Arctic.…北极的冬天。


An onlooker is someone who watches an event take place but does not take part in it. 旁观者
onlooker |英 ['ɒnlʊkə]  美 ['ɑnlʊkɚ]| n. 旁观者；观众（等于spectator）/
A handful of onlookers stand in the field watching.少数旁观者站在场地里观看。
