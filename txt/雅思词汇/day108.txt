If something such as someone's behaviour grates on you or grates, it makes you feel annoyed. 使恼火
When something grates, it rubs against something else, making a harsh, unpleasant sound. 发出吱吱嘎嘎的摩擦声
If you grate food such as cheese or carrots, you rub it over a metal tool called a grater so that the food is cut into very small pieces. 磨碎 (食物)
A grate is a framework of metal bars in a fireplace, which holds the wood or coal. 炉栅
grate |英 [greɪt]  美 [ɡret]| vt. 装格栅于；磨擦/vi. 发摩擦声/n. 壁炉；格栅/n. (Grate)人名；(瑞典)格拉特/
His manner always grated on me.他的举止总是令我很恼火。
His chair grated as he got to his feet.当他站起身时，椅子发出了嘎嘎声响。
Grate the cheese into a mixing bowl.将干酪磨碎放进搅拌碗里。
A wood fire burned in the grate.柴火在炉栅里燃烧。


If a doctor or a nurse administers a drug, they give it to a patient. (医生、护士) 派发 (药物)
If someone administers something such as a country, the law, or a test, they take responsibility for organizing and supervising it. 监管 (国家、法律、考试等)
administer |英 [əd'mɪnɪstə]  美 [əd'mɪnɪstɚ]| vt. 管理；执行；给予/vi. 给予帮助；执行遗产管理人的职责；担当管理人/
The physician may prescribe but not administer the drug.内科医师可以开处方但不可发药。
The plan calls for the UN to administer the country until elections can be held.该计划呼吁联合国监管该国直至选举可以举行。


To restrain something that is growing or increasing means to prevent it from getting too large. 限制
If you restrain an emotion or you restrain yourself from doing something, you prevent yourself from showing that emotion or doing what you wanted or intended to do. 抑制
If you restrain someone, you stop them from doing what they intended or wanted to do, usually by using your physical strength. 制止
restrain |英 [rɪ'streɪn]  美 [rɪ'stren]| vt. 抑制，控制；约束；制止/
The radical 500-day plan was very clear on how it intended to try to restrain inflation.这项激进的500天计划在其想要如何试图限制通货膨胀上是非常清晰的。
She was unable to restrain her desperate anger.她无法抑制她那不顾一切的愤怒。
Wally gripped my arm, partly to restrain me and partly to reassure me.沃利抓住了我的胳膊，部分是想制止我，部分是想安慰我。

You can refer to something that you can partly see through, such as a mist, as a veil. (薄雾等) 半透明物
You can refer to something that hides or partly hides a situation or activity as a veil. 掩饰物
A veil is a piece of thin soft cloth that women sometimes wear over their heads and that can also cover their face. 面纱

If one thing is eclipsed by a second thing that is bigger, newer, or more important than it, the first thing is no longer noticed because the second thing gets all the attention. 使黯然失色
An eclipse of the sun is an occasion when the moon is between the earth and the sun, so that for a short time you cannot see part or all of the sun. An eclipse of the moon is an occasion when the earth is between the sun and the moon, so that for a short time you cannot see part or all of the moon. (日、月) 食
eclipse |英 [ɪ'klɪps]  美 [ɪ'klɪps]| vt. 使黯然失色；形成蚀/n. 日蚀，月蚀；黯然失色/

...the solar eclipse on May 21.…5月21日的日食。
...an eclipse of the sun.…一次日食。


If you say scarcely had one thing happened when something else happened, you mean that the first event was followed immediately by the second. 刚…就…
You can use scarcely to say that something is not true or is not the case, in a humorous or critical way. 决不
You use scarcely to emphasize that something is only just true or only just the case. 几乎不; 简直不
