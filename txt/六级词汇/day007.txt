Definite evidence or information is true, rather than being someone's opinion or guess. 确切的
If something such as a decision or an arrangement is definite, it is firm and clear, and unlikely to be changed. 明确的
definite |英 ['defɪnɪt]  美 ['dɛfɪnət]| adj. 一定的；确切的/
Mary is very definite about this.玛丽对此非常肯定。
There has already been a definite improvement.已经有了显著的改善。
We didn't have any definite proof.我们没有任何确凿的证据。
She made no definite plans for her future.她对自己的未来没有明确的计划。
It's too soon to give a definite answer.现在给予明确答复还为时尚早。


A table in a restaurant or a seat in a theatre that is reserved is being kept for someone rather than given or sold to anyone else. 预订的
Someone who is reserved keeps their feelings hidden. 矜持的
reserved |英 [rɪ'zɜːvd]  美 [rɪ'zɝvd]| adj. 保留的，预订的；缄默的，冷淡的，高冷的；包租的/
Seats, or sometimes entire tables, were reserved.座位，有时候全部的桌子，都被预订了。
He was unemotional, quiet, and reserved.他不动感情、安静而矜持。


Your affections are your feelings of love or fondness for someone. 感情
If you regard someone or something with affection, you like them and are fond of them. 喜爱
affection |英 [ə'fekʃ(ə)n]  美 [ə'fɛkʃən]| n. 喜爱，感情；影响；感染/
Caroline is the object of his affections.卡罗琳是他感情的归属。
She thought of him with affection.她怀着喜爱想起了他。
